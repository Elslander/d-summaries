## Primary Research

- Field research
- Qualitative and quantitative data

Collecting data and experiences about behavior, feelings, common characteristics of a group of people in real life.

**Principles and methodologies**:

- Observation
- Surveys
- Interviews
- Cultural probe
- Rapid ethnography
- Focus group
- Contextual enquiry
- Control group analysis

## Surveys

- Quantitative

A form on paper or online. Anonymity is important. Is quick and easy to set up.

**Types of surveys**:

- **Online surveys**
- Paper surveys
- Mobile surveys
- Telephone interviews
- Online polls
- **Social media polls** (stories)
- Anything that can generate data

### Speak your audience's language

Empathise!

- Avoid jargon & acronyms
- Do not be vague
- Frame of reference

**Jargon & acronyms**: Avoid technical terms and jargon.

**Avoid vagueness**: Use terms that have the same specific meaning to all respondents.

**Frame of reference**: Make sure everyone understands the question in the same way.

### The structure

- Intro of survey
- Start - middle - end
- Open and closed questions

#### Intro

1. Who are you?
2. Why are youb asking for input?
3. How will you use the gathered data? (privacy)
4. How long will it take to complete the survey? Are there benefits?
5. Thank you & CTA

**Why?**

Respondents are more likely to answer your survey questions if they can see value in them.

Explain the purpose of your survey right at the beginning.

- to improve customer service
- to make a product better
- to make their life more pleasant or easier

#### Beginning - middle - end

**Logical Flow**:

- Begin with some broad questions on the topic.
- Group questions into logical sections with a sensible order often starting general and moving to specific.
- Ask the heavy questions in the middle.

**Multiple Pages**:

- Break long surveys into multiple pages to make the survey feel less overwhelming.

**Demographixs at the end?**:

- Place demographic questions at the end of the survey, as they are often considered more sensitive.

#### Types of questions

**Closed questions**:

- quantitative data
- says nothing about the context, motivation, the cause of something
- yes/no, checkbox, radio buttons
- graph, compare percentages

**Open questions**:

- qualitative data
- on behavior action, how the user thinks about a problem
- text box
- more time needed to analyse
