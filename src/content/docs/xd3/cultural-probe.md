> The cultural probe is a qualitative method.

Understand challenges, goals, needs of the user at the beginning of a design process/idea phase.

Participants document and reflext on aspects of their lives over a longer period of time.

## How to

In its pure form, the cultural probe is a **box**.

This box contains:

- small activities
- pictures
- story
- game
- ...

What the box is for:

- keep track of daily activities
- keep track of feelings
- keep track of emotions
- keep track of experiences

When to do the box:

- one time per day
- when they want

## Example box

**Instructions**

**Daily Activities**

1. Journal
2. Surprise eggs
3. Capture the moment

**Other**

- Product associations
- Life map: past, present, future
- Relate to a product and the golden circle

## Cultural probe

### Is for

- You can't get to people.
- It's a difficult topic and people have a hard time chatting about it.
- Talking is not possible anyway (disability,...).
- People are better off expressing themselves with images, stories,...
- **Early in the project to uncover values, hopes and fears to inform your project direction**.

### Is not for

- You want to be able to follow up on a particular topic.
- You want interaction.
- Is harder to get insights. Good interpretation is needed.

## Cultural prove VS Survey

| Cultural probe                                                                                                                                                      | Survey                                               |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| Understanding mid-term behaviors or changes in behavior, attitude, or feelings over time.                                                                           | Offers a snapshot.                                   |
| In a cultural probe you can ask the same questions multiple times over the course of a few weeks and observe changes when the particpants' environment is modified. | People give an answer to experiences of that moment. |

## Preparation

1. **Who**: To whom will you submit the cultural probe?
1. **What**: What do you cant to find out?
1. **How**: How are you going to 'probe' your audience?
1. **Where**: Where can / must the people perform the cultural probe?
1. **Time**: How much time do they get in total and how much per item from the box?
